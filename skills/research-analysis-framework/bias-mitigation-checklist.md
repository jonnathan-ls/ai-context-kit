# Bias Mitigation Checklist - Quality Gates & Objectivity Controls

## Purpose

Systematic checks to ensure analysis is objective, not biased. Cognitive biases are human nature; these controls help surface and correct them before finalizing recommendations.

---

## Pre-Analysis: Planning Gates

### Question 1: Am I Starting Unbiased?

**Confirmation Test**:
- [ ] What do I *want* the answer to be? (be honest)
- [ ] What answer would I *like* to recommend?
- [ ] Am I emotionally invested in an outcome?

**Why This Matters**: If you want a specific answer, you'll find evidence to support it. Awareness is first defense.

**Mitigation**: 
- If you notice bias, explicitly work against it
- Assign devil's advocate to challenge your thinking
- Plan to seek contradictory evidence
- Document your starting assumptions

### Question 2: What Assumptions Am I Starting With?

**Assumption Test**:
- [ ] What do I believe to be true without evidence?
- [ ] Which of these assumptions could be wrong?
- [ ] What would change if this assumption breaks?

**Examples of Common Assumptions**:
```
❌ "Project management is straightforward" (assumes standard conditions)
❌ "Costs follow historical patterns" (assumes stable markets)
❌ "Contractor will be professional" (assumes competence)
❌ "Timeline is predictable" (assumes no surprises)
```

**Mitigation**:
- Make assumptions explicit
- Validate assumptions with sources
- Plan contingencies if assumptions break
- Flag high-risk assumptions for team discussion

## During Analysis: Gathering Evidence

### Gate 1: Source Quality

**Source Selection Test**:
- [ ] Am I relying too heavily on one source?
- [ ] Do I have TIER 1 sources for critical claims?
- [ ] Am I dismissing sources that contradict my hypothesis?
- [ ] Are my sources credible and independent?

**Common Bias**: Availability bias (using easy-to-find sources, not best sources)

**Mitigation**:
- Seek TIER 1 sources first
- Explicitly search for contradictory evidence
- Document why you excluded sources (if you did)
- Get multiple independent sources for critical findings

### Gate 2: Interpretation

**Interpretation Test**:
For each piece of evidence, ask:
- [ ] What does this evidence actually say?
- [ ] Am I reading it correctly?
- [ ] Could someone interpret this differently?
- [ ] Am I over-stating what this evidence proves?

**Example of Misinterpretation**:
```
SOURCE: "Some contractors report difficulty hiring"
WRONG INTERPRETATION: "Labor shortage is critical"
RIGHT INTERPRETATION: "Hiring difficulty reported, but extent unclear"
```

**Mitigation**:
- Quote sources directly; don't paraphrase
- Acknowledge alternative interpretations
- Don't over-claim what evidence proves
- Get peer review of interpretations

### Gate 3: Evidence Balance

**Balance Test**:
- [ ] Are my sources balanced (mix of TIER levels)?
- [ ] Do I have evidence for AND against my hypothesis?
- [ ] Am I giving equal weight to contrary evidence?
- [ ] Would a skeptical reader be convinced?

**Common Bias**: Confirmation bias (looking for support, ignoring contradictions)

**Mitigation**:
- Actively search for contradictory evidence
- Make it a team sport (assign someone to argue opposite)
- Weigh contrary evidence fairly
- Document contradictions and how you resolved them

## Before Recommendations: Analysis Gates

### Gate 1: Viabitily Scoring

**Scoring Test**:
- [ ] Did I score all 6 dimensions independently?
- [ ] Or did my overall opinion influence the scores?
- [ ] Are scores justified by evidence (not wishful thinking)?
- [ ] Did I anchor on first number I heard?

**Common Bias**: Anchoring bias (first number, estimate becomes reference)

**Mitigation**:
- Calculate scores bottom-up (facts) not top-down (opinion)
- Use multiple estimation methods
- Compare to benchmarks
- Don't let first estimate overly influence final score

### Gate 2: Confidence Levels

**Confidence Test**:
- [ ] Did I honestly assess confidence?
- [ ] Am I claiming more certainty than evidence supports?
- [ ] Did I assign 🟢 HIGH without TIER 1 sources?
- [ ] Did I downgrade confidence on findings I don't like?
- [ ] Am I consistent in confidence assignment (same evidence = same confidence)?

**Common Bias**: Overconfidence bias (claiming certainty we don't have)

**Mitigation**:
- Use defined confidence criteria (tie to source tiers)
- Red-team review of confidence assignments
- Look for upward bias (claiming too high confidence)
- Look for downward bias (confidence lower when finding is unwelcome)

### Gate 3: Recommendation Logic

**Logic Test**:
For each recommendation, trace:
```
Recommendation
  ← Based on findings
    ← Which came from evidence
      ← Which came from sources

Does each link hold up?
```

**Common Bias**: Motivated reasoning (conclusions drive findings, not vice versa)

**Example of Bad Logic**:
```
RECOMMENDATION: "This contractor is great"
FINDING: "Contractor has experience" (weak evidence)
SOURCE: "Contractor told me they're experienced" (circular)
ISSUE: Recommendation not justified by evidence
```

**Mitigation**:
- Reverse-check recommendations
- Remove recommendations not justified by findings
- Strengthen evidence for critical recommendations
- Get peer review of logic chain

## Specific Cognitive Biases: Detection & Mitigation

### BIAS 1: Confirmation Bias

**What It Is**: Seeking evidence that confirms your hypothesis; ignoring contradictions

**Detection**: 
- [ ] Did I actively search for contradictory evidence?
- [ ] Or did I stop searching once I found supporting evidence?
- [ ] How many sources contradict my conclusion?

**Mitigation**:
- Deliberately search for contrary evidence
- Assign devil's advocate
- Document contradictions
- For every pro-argument, find a con-argument

**Test Question**: "What evidence would convince me I'm wrong?"

---

### BIAS 2: Anchoring Bias

**What It Is**: First number becomes reference; later estimates overly influenced by it

**Detection**:
- [ ] Did I use multiple estimation methods?
- [ ] Or did I accept first estimate and adjust slightly?
- [ ] Why are my three cost estimates all close?

**Mitigation**:
- Estimate independently before hearing others' estimates
- Use different methods (top-down, bottom-up, benchmarks)
- Compare final estimate to benchmarks
- Document which method you trust most and why

**Test Question**: "If I estimated this fresh (ignoring prior estimates), what would I get?"

---

### BIAS 3: Optimism/Pessimism Bias

**What It Is**: Over- or under-estimating viability based on emotional bias

**Detection**:
- [ ] Am I being unrealistically hopeful about this project?
- [ ] Or unrealistically fearful?
- [ ] Are my estimates aligned with benchmarks?
- [ ] Or am I out of bounds on either side?

**Mitigation**:
- Compare viability scores to benchmarks
- Red-team review (have skeptics challenge estimates)
- Conduct scenario analysis (best, worst, realistic)
- Use conservative assumptions when uncertain

**Test Question**: "How do my estimates compare to similar projects?
Am I in typical range or outlier?"

---

### BIAS 4: Availability Bias

**What It Is**: Recent/memorable examples overly influence judgment; forget base rates

**Detection**:
- [ ] Am I basing statement on memorable example (one project)?
- [ ] Do I have statistical evidence?
- [ ] What's the base rate (typical case)?

**Mitigation**:
- Prefer statistical evidence over anecdotes
- Look up base rates
- Weight TIER 1/2 sources over TIER 3/4 stories
- Document using data, not anecdotes

**Test Question**: "Is this typical or exceptional? What does base rate data say?"

---

### BIAS 5: Authority Bias

**What It Is**: Over-weighting expert opinion; accepting assertions without evidence

**Detection**:
- [ ] Did I accept expert statement as fact?
- [ ] Or did I ask for evidence supporting the statement?
- [ ] Is expert's opinion in their domain?

**Mitigation**:
- Distinguish opinion from evidence
- Ask experts for supporting data
- Check if expert is truly authoritative in this domain
- Compare expert opinions (do they agree?)

**Test Question**: "What evidence supports what this expert claims?
Could someone disagree?"

---

### BIAS 6: Groupthink

**What It Is**: Team consensus overly influences analysis; dissent suppressed

**Detection**:
- [ ] Did team quickly agree (surprisingly so)?
- [ ] Did anyone voice doubts? Were they heard?
- [ ] Are there alternative interpretations we didn't discuss?

**Mitigation**:
- Explicitly encourage dissent
- Red-team analysis (assign someone to argue opposite)
- Get input from outside team
- Document alternative viewpoints
- Make contrary opinion respectable

**Test Question**: "What would a skeptic say about this analysis?"

---

### BIAS 7: Recency Bias

**What It Is**: Recent events overly influence judgment; long-term patterns underweighted

**Detection**:
- [ ] Am I basing forecast on recent data?
- [ ] What does longer-term data show?
- [ ] Is recent trend abnormal or continuation?

**Mitigation**:
- Always check historical data
- Compare recent trend to 5-year pattern
- Use longer time horizon in forecasts
- Note if current conditions are unusual

**Test Question**: "Is this recent trend new, or continuation of long pattern?"

---

## Pre-Delivery: Final Bias Audit

Before finalizing analysis, conduct this checklist:

### Confirmation Bias Check
- [ ] Did I look for contradictory evidence?
- [ ] Did I document contradictions I found?
- [ ] Did I give fair weight to contrary evidence?
- [ ] Would a skeptic say I'm cherry-picking?

### Anchoring Bias Check
- [ ] Did I estimate using multiple methods?
- [ ] Are final estimates aligned with benchmarks?
- [ ] Or am I outlier (why?)?
- [ ] Did I reverse-check my numbers?

### Optimism/Pessimism Check
- [ ] Are my viability scores realistic?
- [ ] Too high (wishful thinking)?
- [ ] Too low (excessive caution)?
- [ ] Compared to similar projects?

### Availability Bias Check
- [ ] Am I basing statements on base rates (data)?
- [ ] Or memorable examples (anecdotes)?
- [ ] Do I have statistical evidence?
- [ ] Did I weight TIER 1/2 sources appropriately?

### Authority Bias Check
- [ ] Did I accept expert opinions without question?
- [ ] Do experts have supporting evidence?
- [ ] Do multiple experts agree?
- [ ] Or is expert opinion just opinion?

### Groupthink Check
- [ ] Did we encourage dissent?
- [ ] Did we get outside perspectives?
- [ ] Did we challenge consensus?
- [ ] What would a skeptic say?

### Recency Bias Check
- [ ] Did I look at long-term trends?
- [ ] Or just recent events?
- [ ] Is recent trend abnormal?
- [ ] Does historical data support conclusion?

## Red Team Exercise

Assign someone (different from analyst) to challenge analysis:

### Red Team Questions

**On Methodology**:
- [ ] What assumptions drove the analysis?
- [ ] Which assumptions are weakest?
- [ ] What if [critical assumption] is wrong?

**On Sources**:
- [ ] Are sources credible?
- [ ] Why are TIER 3/4 sources used for critical claims?
- [ ] What sources are missing?
- [ ] Are there contradictory sources we ignored?

**On Evidence**:
- [ ] Is evidence sufficient for conclusion?
- [ ] Could evidence be interpreted differently?
- [ ] What's the weakest part of the argument?
- [ ] What would change your conclusion?

**On Scoring**:
- [ ] Are viability scores justified?
- [ ] Are scores inflated because analyst wants project to succeed?
- [ ] Are scores deflated to be "safe"?
- [ ] How do scores compare to benchmarks?

**On Recommendations**:
- [ ] Is recommendation justified by findings?
- [ ] Do findings actually support recommendation?
- [ ] What alternative recommendations could you make?
- [ ] What conditions would change recommendation?

Document findings:

```
BIAS AUDIT RESULTS

Strengths (where we did well)
├─ [Source diversity good; TIER 1 sources present]
├─ [Evidence balanced; contradictions surfaced]
└─ [Multiple estimation methods used]

Weaknesses (where bias may exist)
├─ [Risk: Optimism bias in timeline estimate; analyst rose-tinted]
├─ [Concern: TIER 3 sources used for cost; should lock TIER 1 quotes]
└─ [Gap: Contradictory evidence on contractor reliability not fully addressed]

Mitigations Applied
├─ [Red team reviewed scores; no major inflation detected]
├─ [Confidence levels conservative; no unjustified 🟢 HIGH]
└─ [Contingency built in to protect against optimism]

Confidence in Analysis
Overall quality: GOOD (bias well-managed)
Areas of lowest confidence: [contractor quality, long-term market trends]
Recommendation robustness: Changes to [assumption X] would alter recommendation
```

## Rules for Bias-Free Analysis

1. **Document your assumptions**: List what you're taking as given
2. **Actively seek contradiction**: Don't stop searching when you find support
3. **Use TIER 1 sources**: Don't rely on TIER 3/4 for critical claims
4. **Compare to benchmarks**: Are your estimates in normal range?
5. **Show confidence levels**: Don't claim certainty you don't have
6. **Get peer review**: Have skeptic review your logic
7. **Use multiple methods**: Triangulation increases confidence
8. **Accept uncertainty**: Some things can't be known; say so
9. **Update with new evidence**: Change conclusions if evidence changes
10. **Be transparent**: Show your reasoning so reader can judge

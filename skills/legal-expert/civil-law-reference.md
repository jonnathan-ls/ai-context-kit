# Civil Law Reference - Brazilian Legislation

## Instruments & Foundations

### Brazilian Civil Code (CC - Law nº 10.406/2002)

| Articles | Topic | Description |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| **Art. 104-106** | Requirements of Legal Act | Capable agent, lawful object, prescribed form |
| **Art. 107-115** | Legal Transaction | Nullity, voidability, interpretation |
| **Art. 421-422** | Freedom and Good Faith Clause | Contractor cannot act contrary to good faith |
| **Art. 423-424** | Contract Interpretation | Clear contract does not admit interpretation |
| **Art. 425-427** | Binding Force | Contract binds parties to agreed terms |
| **Art. 428-435** | Proposal & Acceptance | Conditions for contract formation |
| **Art. 436-439** | Clause Interpretation | Ambiguity interpreted against drafter |
| **Art. 440-456** | Vices of Consent | Error, fraud, coercion |
| **Art. 466-489** | Prescription & Decadence | Deadlines for legal action |

### General Principles of Contracts

| Principle | Application | Implication |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Freedom of Contract** | Parties define terms | Limited by law (public order, good faith) |
| **Objective Good Faith** | Standard of honest conduct | Abusive clauses are void |
| **Binding Force** | Contract is law between parties | Only rescinds by consent/law |
| **Material Equivalence** | Performances must be proportional | Excessive disproportion can void |
| **Pre-contractual** | Diligence before signing | Informs/clarifies before |

## Common Risk Areas in Civil Contracts

### 1. Ambiguity & Interpretation

**Principal Rule (Art. 423 CC):**
- Clear contract does not admit interpretation
- Ambiguity interpreted **against the drafter**
- Generic clauses are interpreted restrictively

**Red Flags:**
- "General services" (without definition)
- "Value to be determined later"
- "As needed"
- References to non-attached documents

---

### 2. Abusive Clauses (Consumer Law - Law 8.078/90)

**Prohibitions (even in B2B contracts):**
- Undue exclusion/limitation of liability
- Unilateral renegotiation obligation
- Unilateral cancellation without cause
- Excessive and disproportionate penalties

**Consequence:** Automatic nullity

---

### 3. Vices of Consent

| Flaw | Definition | Effect |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Error** | Ignorance about essential fact | Voidability |
| **Fraud** | Deliberate action to induce error | Voidability + indemnity |
| **Coercion** | Psychological pressure/threat | Voidability |

---

### 4. Deadlines & Prescription

| Type | Deadline | Count From |
|------|----------|------------|
| **General Personal Action** | 10 years | Date of act |
| **Debt Collection** | 5 years | Debt maturity |
| **Civil Liability** | 3 years | Knowledge of damage |
| **Rescissory Action** | 2 years | Judgment finality |

**Important:** Deadlines begin from contract termination (if rescission/conclusion).

---

### 5. Liability & Indemnification

**Civil Liability:**
- **Obligation of result:** Contractor liable for result (ex: delivery)
- **Obligation of means:** Contractor liable for effort (ex: consulting)

**Indemnification:**
- **Actual damage:** Direct prejudice
- **Lucro cessante:** Forgone gain
- **Moral damage:** Suffering, embarrassment

---

### 6. Contract Rescission

| Type | Situation | Effect |
|------|-----------|--------|
| **Mutual Rescission** | Both parties agree | Contract never existed (ex tunc) |
| **Unilateral Rescission** | Contractual clause allows | Contract validation altered |
| **Resolution** | Breach of obligation | Contract resolves retroactively |
| **Nullity** | Fundamental flaw | Contract never existed |

## Specific Clauses & Their Legal Status

### ✅ Generally Valid (when well-drafted)

- **Liability Exclusion:** Valid if reasonable and not abusive
- **Liability Limitation:** Valid in B2B contracts (non-consumer)
- **Confidentiality:** Entirely valid
- **Intellectual Property:** Valid, better if explicit
- **Applicable Law & Jurisdiction:** Valid for commercial contracts

### ❌ Generally Void (or questionable)

- **Absolute Liability Nullity:** Abusive, voided
- **Unlimited Unilateral Alteration:** Contrary to good faith
- **Excessive Penalties:** Can be reduced judicially
- **Unilateral Cancellation Without Cause:** Abusive if no deadline/indemnity
- **Waiver of Fundamental Rights:** Void

## Negotiation & Modification Strategies

### Leverage Points (Use in Negotiation)

1. **Symmetry:** "Clause X applies only to one party. Must be symmetric."
2. **Proportionality:** "Penalty of R$X is disproportional to risk."
3. **Clarity:** "Term Y is ambiguous. Needs clear definitions in Annexes."
4. **Market:** "Market doesn't accept this clause in similar contracts."
5. **Risk Sharing:** "Risk should be distributed between parties."

### Standard Protective Clauses

- **Mutual Liability Limitation:** Both parties limited
- **Indemnification:** Each party indemnifies for own acts
- **Force Majeure:** Exonerates in extraordinary cases
- **Confidentiality:** Protects sensitive information
- **Rescission Notice:** Guarantees transition time
